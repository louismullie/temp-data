## Mechanism of action relevant to female urinary retention

Tamsulosin selectively blocks alpha-1A receptors in the lower urinary tract, reducing urethral resistance and improving flow. In women, it lowers urethral pressure and may help **functional or idiopathic outlet obstruction** [^111k9wnY], but it is **not effective for mechanical obstruction** (e.g. prolapse, strictures) [^111dsHWs].

### Postoperative urinary retention (POUR)

## Safety and tolerability in females

Tamsulosin is generally well tolerated; monitor for **orthostatic hypotension** [^112JCzBy] and dizziness, particularly at initiation or when used with PDE5 inhibitors [^111bhsw4]. Other adverse effects include headache, nasal congestion, and rare allergic reactions [^111bhsw4].

## Contraindications and precautions

Contraindications include **hypersensitivity to tamsulosin** or its components. Use caution in patients with sulfa allergy, when coadministered with CYP3A4/2D6 inhibitors, and during cataract or glaucoma surgery due to the risk of **intraoperative floppy iris syndrome (IFIS)** [^111bhsw4] [^116Zjmm8].

### Effect of preoperative tamsulosin on postoperative urinary retention prevention after sling placement: A randomized controlled trial [^112v7AW7]. International Urogynecology Journal (2025). High credibility.

The objective of this study was to evaluate whether preoperative administration of tamsulosin would decrease the frequency of postoperative urinary retention (POUR) after mid-urethral sling (MUS) placement.

- **Methods**: This was a prospective randomized, double-blinded, placebo-controlled trial of patients with stress urinary incontinence (SUI) who underwent elective MUS placement at a single institution. Patients scheduled to undergo MUS placement consented, were enrolled, and were randomized to receive either a single tablet of tamsulosin 0.4 mg or placebo in the preoperative holding area on the day of their surgery. The rate of POUR after administration of tamsulosin compared with placebo was evaluated using appropriate statistical methods. The sample size was calculated to include 160 patients.

- **Results**: A total of 161 patients (81 placebo, 80 tamsulosin) were analyzed. The incidence of POUR was similar between the tamsulosin and placebo groups (17.7% vs. 19.8%, p = 0.7420). Secondary outcomes, including unplanned admissions, urinary tract infections (UTIs), and hypotension, did not differ significantly between groups. A subgroup analysis of patients undergoing MUS without concomitant prolapse surgery suggested a trend toward lower POUR rates in the tamsulosin group (7.5% vs. 16.7%, p = 0.142), although this was not statistically significant.

- **Conclusion**: These results suggest that single-dose preoperative tamsulosin might not have an effect on postoperative urinary retention after MUS placement, with or without concomitant reconstructive pelvic surgery.
