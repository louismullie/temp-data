The **Cognitive Assessment System (CAS)** is the test that provides information on the four cognitive abilities identified by Luria (1980): planning, attention, simultaneous processing, and sequential processing. The CAS is explicitly based on Luria's neuropsychological theory and the PASS model, yielding separate scores for each process. The other options do not specifically measure these four abilities: the Slosson Intelligence Test Primary is a brief screening tool, the Kuhlman-Anderson Test is an older group ability test, and the Woodcock-Johnson III focuses on academic achievement and cognitive abilities but not the PASS processes.

## Theoretical foundation of the cognitive assessment system

The CAS is grounded in Luria's neuropsychological theory, which identifies four core cognitive processes: planning, attention, simultaneous processing, and sequential processing. These processes are integral to cognitive functioning and are assessed independently by the CAS, providing a comprehensive profile of an individual's cognitive strengths and weaknesses [^b3bd5dbe].
