## Clinical features of progressive pseudorheumatoid dysplasia

PPRD is characterized by the following **clinical and radiographic features**:

- **Progressive joint stiffness**: Particularly affecting the interphalangeal joints, leading to flexion contractures and limited range of motion [^a0a320c4].

- **Enlarged interphalangeal joints**: Due to metaphyseal bony overgrowth, often mimicking inflammatory arthritis [^3f485b1a].

- **Normal inflammatory markers**: ESR and CRP are typically normal, distinguishing PPRD from inflammatory arthritides [^e29e9ee7].

- **Radiographic findings**: Platyspondyly (flattened vertebral bodies), epimetaphyseal dysplasia, and progressive joint degeneration are characteristic [^8bbeb2ba].

- **Genetic confirmation**: Mutations in the WISP3 gene (CCN6) confirm the diagnosis [^8bbeb2ba].
