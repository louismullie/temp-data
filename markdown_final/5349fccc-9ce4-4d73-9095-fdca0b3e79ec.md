## Pharmacological treatment options

### Selective estrogen receptor modulators (SERMs)

Among SERMs, **Tamoxifen** is the first-line medical therapy for gynecomastia, particularly in pubertal and anti-androgen–induced cases [^1155LtRs]. It competitively inhibits estrogen receptors in breast tissue, reducing glandular proliferation and tenderness [^111uDJQR]. Clinical studies show 80–90% response rates, with rapid symptom relief and minimal adverse effects (hot flashes, nausea, rare thromboembolism) [^116rDQmc]. Raloxifene has **limited evidence**; small studies suggest benefit, but it is not first-line due to fewer data and less established efficacy than tamoxifen [^notfound].

---

### Aromatase inhibitors

Among aromatase inhibitors, **Anastrozole and letrozole** reduce estrogen synthesis but have not shown efficacy in randomized trials for pubertal gynecomastia [^116wHkve] [^1125Myzb]. Guidelines do not recommend them for routine management [^114M9j1S].

---

### Androgens

Testosterone is indicated only for **confirmed hypogonadism** and may exacerbate gynecomastia if estradiol is elevated; monitor estradiol and adjust therapy as needed [^114cMtCS] [^113yfTKp]. Non-aromatizable androgens (e.g. danazol) have limited evidence and are not recommended due to adverse effects and lack of robust data [^114M9j1S].

---

### Clomiphene citrate

Clomiphene citrate has **limited evidence**; small studies suggest potential benefit, but it is not first-line and should be considered only when other options are unsuitable [^11673vQQ].

## Factors influencing treatment choice

- **Etiology**: Identify and treat underlying causes (medications, hormonal disorders, systemic diseases) [^112cSCfZ].

- **Duration**: Medical therapy is most effective in early, active disease; long-standing fibrotic gynecomastia is less responsive [^113XNRWC].

- **Patient preference**: Consider patient goals, tolerance, and risk profile [^11415pnt].

- **Guideline recommendations**: Follow current guidelines and expert consensus [^114M9j1S].

## Limitations and side effects

- **Tamoxifen**: Hot flashes, nausea, rare thromboembolism.

- **Aromatase inhibitors**: Ineffective, potential bone density effects with long-term use.

- **Testosterone**: Risk of worsening gynecomastia, erythrocytosis, prostate effects [^116iwu4T].

- **Clomiphene**: Limited data, potential ocular side effects.

---

## Alternative treatments

Surgery (subcutaneous mastectomy, liposuction) is preferred for **long-standing, fibrotic, or medically refractory gynecomastia** [^112j3Auk] [^112NcYjr]. Radiation therapy has a limited role, mainly for prophylaxis in anti-androgen–induced gynecomastia [^111RhLeN].

### Clomiphene citrate: a potential alternative for testosterone therapy in hypogonadal males [^115TzjwF]. Endocrinology, Diabetes & Metabolism (2023). Medium credibility.

1 INTRODUCTION

Male hypogonadism is a biochemical and clinical testosterone deficiency syndrome with a prevalence of symptomatic hypogonadism ranging between 2.1% and 5.7% in males aged above 30 years of age.,

Prevalence increases with age and certain comorbidities, such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes mellitus (DM), obesity and malignancies. Decreased libido, lack of energy, mood changes, decreased muscle mass, and erectile dysfunction are common hypogonadal symptoms. Testosterone therapy (TTh) is the treatment of first choice for male hypogonadism. However, exogenous testosterone leads to negative feedback on the hypothalamic–pituitary‐gonadal (HPG) axis, causing suppression of endogenous testosterone production and spermatogenesis. Other side effects are, changed lipid serum, polycythaemia and gynecomastia.

Clomiphene citrate (CC) is an alternative off‐label pharmacological treatment for a certain group of males with hypogonadism. CC is a selective oestrogen receptor modulator, occupying oestrogen receptors in the hypothalamus and pituitary gland, leading to increased secretion of luteinizing hormone (LH) and follicle stimulating hormone (FSH) and so, stimulating testicular endogenous testosterone production and preserving spermatogenesis.

Since the 1960s, CC has been often used for ovulation induction in females.
