Clinicians should use the general code **E63.8** (Other specified nutritional deficiencies) to document EPA deficiency, and may add Z13.21 (Encounter for screening for nutritional disorder) if screening is performed. ## ICD-10 coding for EPA deficiency

ICD-10 does not include a specific code for EPA deficiency. Clinicians should use **E63.8** (Other specified nutritional deficiencies), which applies when a deficiency is specified but **does not have a dedicated ICD-10 code**; EPA deficiency falls into this category.

## Clinical documentation requirements

When coding EPA deficiency in ICD-10, clinicians must provide **clear, detailed documentation** to support the diagnosis [^116L1QL2]. Include the following in the patient's medical record:

- **Clinical signs and symptoms**: Document any clinical manifestations associated with EPA deficiency, such as inflammation, cardiovascular symptoms, or neurological symptoms.

- **Laboratory findings**: Include laboratory results indicating low EPA levels, such as plasma or erythrocyte membrane fatty acid analysis [^115jbokf].

- **Dietary history**: Document the patient's dietary intake, specifically noting low consumption of omega-3 fatty acids or fish oil supplements [^1132gu2K].

- **Risk factors**: Identify any risk factors for EPA deficiency, such as strict vegetarian or vegan diets, malabsorption syndromes, or chronic inflammatory conditions [^114hbVYM].

## Clinical implications of EPA deficiency

EPA deficiency can have **significant clinical implications**, including:

- **Cardiovascular health**: EPA deficiency is associated with increased cardiovascular risk, including higher triglyceride levels, inflammation, and potential cardiovascular events [^113cFaNU] [^115DU5Kf].

- **Inflammation**: EPA has anti-inflammatory properties, and deficiency may contribute to chronic inflammatory conditions [^115H6CAJ].

- **Neurological health**: EPA deficiency may impact neurological function, although DHA is more prominently associated with neurological development and function [^111JxdXW] [^1126o7Qb].

## Treatment and management strategies

Management of EPA deficiency involves **dietary and supplemental interventions**:

- **Dietary intake**: Increase consumption of omega-3-rich foods, such as fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines) or fish oil supplements [^1132gu2K].

- **Supplementation**: EPA supplementation may be recommended, particularly for individuals with hypertriglyceridemia or cardiovascular risk factors [^1137e2e1] [^1154fSJQ].

- **Monitoring**: Regular monitoring of EPA levels and clinical symptoms is essential to assess treatment effectiveness [^115jbokf].

### The effects of omega-3 fatty acids on neuropsychological functioning and brain morphology in mid-life adults: a randomized clinical trial [^1126o7Qb]. Psychological Medicine (2020). High credibility.
