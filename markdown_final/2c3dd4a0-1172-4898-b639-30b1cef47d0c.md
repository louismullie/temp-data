## Risks of abrupt discontinuation

Abrupt discontinuation of topiramate **can precipitate seizure recurrence** or withdrawal seizures — even in patients without a prior seizure history — and may cause withdrawal symptoms, including anxiety, irritability, insomnia, nausea, headache, and dizziness. Rapid cessation can trigger rebound worsening of the treated condition (e.g. migraines or seizures) [^116dr8zQ].

## Clinical guidelines and recommendations

Clinical guidelines and expert recommendations **consistently advise gradual tapering** of topiramate to minimize risks associated with abrupt discontinuation. Although specific protocols may vary by clinical indication, patient characteristics, and treatment duration, the general consensus is to taper over several weeks [^112gLc1D].

## Factors influencing tapering decisions

Several factors **influence the decision to taper** topiramate and the specific tapering strategy used:

- **Clinical indication**: The underlying condition being treated significantly influences tapering decisions. For example, epilepsy patients require more cautious tapering due to the risk of seizure recurrence [^112gLc1D].

- **Duration of treatment**: Longer treatment duration necessitates slower tapering to minimize withdrawal symptoms and rebound effects [^115N7ebP].

- **Patient-specific factors**: Patient age, comorbidities, and concomitant medications influence tapering decisions. For example, patients with renal impairment [^112Xir3c] may require slower tapering and dose adjustments.

- **Adverse effects**: The presence of significant adverse effects may necessitate more rapid tapering or abrupt discontinuation in severe cases (e.g. Stevens-Johnson syndrome, acute angle-closure glaucoma) [^1132MoGR] [^115Y54J8] [^116Zwagj].

During tapering, **clinicians should closely monitor** patients for:

- **Withdrawal symptoms**: Anxiety, irritability, insomnia, nausea, headache, dizziness.

- **Rebound of underlying condition**: Worsening of migraines, seizures, or psychiatric symptoms [^116dr8zQ].

- **Adverse effects**: Cognitive impairment, mood changes, metabolic acidosis, hyperammonemia [^111muGGn], renal stones [^114pVPV4].

## Special considerations

- **Pregnancy**: Topiramate is associated with teratogenic risks (e.g. oral clefts). Pregnant patients or those planning pregnancy should taper and discontinue topiramate as soon as possible, considering alternative therapies [^113EJ9iV] [^111VuHJH] [^112aQtGT].

- **Renal impairment**: Patients with renal impairment require dose adjustments and slower tapering due to altered drug clearance [^112Xir3c] [^113qigep] [^115rPNyr].

- **Serious adverse reactions**: Abrupt discontinuation is warranted in cases of severe adverse reactions such as Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis, or acute angle-closure glaucoma [^1132MoGR] [^115Y54J8] [^116Zwagj].

## Conclusion and recommendations

Topiramate should always be tapered gradually [^112gLc1D] to minimize the risk of **seizure recurrence, withdrawal symptoms, and rebound of the underlying condition**.
