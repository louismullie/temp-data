The elimination half-life of Pristiq (desvenlafaxine) is approximately **11 hours** in healthy adults, supporting once-daily dosing and predictable steady-state within 4–5 days [^113hX5wf]. In severe renal impairment or end-stage renal disease, the half-life increases to about **22–33 hours**, requiring dose adjustments to 25 mg daily or 50 mg every other day. Hepatic impairment has minimal effect on half-life, so no dose adjustment is needed [^1132gL51] [^111wk3g4]. The half-life guides tapering to reduce discontinuation symptoms and informs washout periods before starting MAOIs [^112PMVSa] [^1176yG9V].

## Pharmacokinetic profile

- **Absorption**: Desvenlafaxine is rapidly absorbed after oral administration, with peak plasma concentrations (Cmax) typically achieved within 7.5 hours.

- **Distribution**: The apparent volume of distribution (Vd) is approximately 3.4 L/kg, indicating moderate tissue distribution.

- **Metabolism**: Desvenlafaxine undergoes minimal hepatic metabolism, primarily via conjugation (glucuronidation) and minor oxidative metabolism by CYP3A4.

- **Excretion**: Approximately 45% of desvenlafaxine is excreted unchanged in urine, with about 19% as glucuronide conjugates and less than 5% as oxidative metabolites [^111wdZBM].

## Clinical implications of half-life

### Dosing frequency and steady-state

The 11-hour half-life supports **once-daily dosing**, maintaining stable plasma concentrations and reducing fluctuations that could lead to side effects or reduced efficacy [^116MSh1s]. Steady state is typically achieved within 4–5 days of consistent daily dosing, allowing predictable therapeutic effects and easier dose adjustments [^111caKLR] [^113hX5wf].

---

### Discontinuation and withdrawal symptoms

The relatively short half-life of desvenlafaxine increases the risk of withdrawal symptoms if the medication is stopped abruptly; common symptoms include dizziness, nausea, headache, irritability, and sensory disturbances. To minimize these symptoms, **gradual dose reduction is recommended**, and the 25 mg tablet formulation is specifically intended for tapering.

---

### Drug interactions

Desvenlafaxine's minimal hepatic metabolism reduces the likelihood of significant drug-drug interactions compared with antidepressants extensively metabolized by CYP enzymes [^111KhBT6]; however, **caution is advised** when co-administering medications that affect renal function, as impaired renal clearance can prolong desvenlafaxine's half-life and increase plasma concentrations [^1132gL51].
