## Microbiology of canine oral and nasal flora

Dogs harbor a diverse oral and nasal microbiota, including aerobic and anaerobic bacteria. The most frequently isolated aerobic organisms include:

- **Pasteurella multocida**: A gram-negative rod commonly found in the oral cavities of dogs and cats. It is a major pathogen in dog bite infections, often causing rapidly progressing cellulitis and abscesses [^790760b1] [^234e3dce].

- **Staphylococcus species**: Including Staphylococcus pseudintermedius, a coagulase-positive staphylococcus commonly found on canine skin and mucosa. It is frequently isolated from dog bite wounds and can cause significant infections, particularly in immunocompromised individuals [^82accc06] [^1d0d705e].

- **Streptococcus species**: Various streptococcal species, including beta-hemolytic streptococci, are also commonly isolated from dog bite wounds, though they are less prevalent than Pasteurella and Staphylococcus species [^790760b1] [^234e3dce].

---

## Prevalence of Staphylococcus and Pasteurella multocida in dog bite wounds

Several studies have documented the high frequency of Staphylococcus and Pasteurella multocida in dog bite wounds:

- **Pasteurella multocida**: Isolated in approximately 50% of infected dog bite wounds, making it the most common pathogen in these infections [^790760b1] [^234e3dce].

- **Staphylococcus species**: Commonly isolated from dog bite wounds, often as part of polymicrobial infections. Staphylococcus pseudintermedius is particularly prevalent in canine-associated infections [^82accc06] [^1d0d705e].

- **Polymicrobial nature**: Dog bite wounds are frequently polymicrobial, with mixed aerobic and anaerobic flora. However, Staphylococcus and Pasteurella multocida consistently predominate among aerobic isolates [^790760b1] [^234e3dce].

## Clinical significance of Staphylococcus and Pasteurella multocida infections

Infections caused by Staphylococcus and Pasteurella multocida can lead to significant morbidity if not promptly recognized and treated:

- **Pasteurella multocida**: Rapidly progressive cellulitis, abscess formation, and systemic infection are common. Infections can escalate quickly, particularly in immunocompromised individuals [^234e3dce] [^50701446].

- **Staphylococcus species**: Staphylococcus pseudintermedius and other staphylococci can cause localized infections, abscesses, and systemic infections, particularly in patients with underlying health conditions [^82accc06] [^1d0d705e].

### Identification of virulent Capnocytophaga canimorsus isolates by capsular typing [^b1bfef35]. Journal of Clinical Microbiology (2017). Low credibility.

Capnocytophaga canimorsus is a dog oral commensal that causes rare but severe infections in humans. C. canimorsus was recently shown to be endowed with a capsular polysaccharide implicated in resistance to the innate immune system of the host. Here, we developed the first C. canimorsus capsular serotyping scheme. We describe nine different serovars (A to I), and this serotyping scheme allowed typing of 25/25 isolates from human infections but only 18/52 isolates from dog mouths, indicating that the repertoire of capsules in the species is vast. However, while only three serovars (A, B, and C) covered 88% of the human isolates tested (22/25), they covered only 7.7% of the dog isolates (4/52). Serovars A, B, and C were found 22.9-, 14.6-, and 4.2-fold more often, respectively, among human isolates than among dog isolates, with no geographical bias, implying that isolates endowed with these three capsular types are more virulent for humans than other isolates. Capsular serotyping would thus allow identification of virulent isolates in dogs, which could contribute to the prevention of these infections. To this end, we developed a PCR typing method based on the amplification of specific capsular genes.
