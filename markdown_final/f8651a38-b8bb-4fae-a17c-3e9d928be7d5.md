The standard of care for hemophilia B prophylaxis includes **factor IX replacement therapy** [^112c8Teu] — regular intravenous infusions of factor IX concentrates to maintain adequate clotting factor levels and prevent bleeding episodes — and **non-factor therapies** such as fitusiran (an antithrombin-directed siRNA) [^115iJRLu] [^1162t3Cy] and concizumab (a TFPI antagonist) [^1158eLMA], which have shown promising results in reducing bleeding episodes without the increased bleeding risk associated with anticoagulants [^116GZABC] [^116TZe5o] [^115RQDqL].

Instead, **individualized prophylaxis regimens** with factor IX replacement therapy [^112c8Teu] or **non-factor therapies** [^115iJRLu] [^116TZe5o] are recommended to prevent and effectively manage bleeding episodes [^114dDAMV].
